## MIT PD Chief de Fava

1. coming to discuss MIT security regarding developments since situation at
   Brown
    1. heavily focused on external threats to MIT students, working on layers of
       security to cover gaps
    2. officers trained at Cambridge police, or worked at different police
       departments
    3. establishing perimeter requires controlling access
    4. cameras on campus are a layer of security, post-incident investigatory
       tool
        1. all cameras on the current market have some AI features, but MIT does
           not use those features for facial recognition / profiling at least
        2. all cameras on campus have to be approved by security
    5. ID / tap access is another layer of security, weakened by people sharing
       IDs
    6. all employees on campus are background checked by private vendor
    7. emergency management is its own section of the department
        1. work on introducing all student to safety plans / emergency training
    8. communicated with Brown police chief after event in December
    9. questions
        1. how is MIT PD planning to respond to ICE?
            1. MITPD does not plan to assist ICE (unless ICE officers are
               assaulted on campus → intervention)
            2. unless ICE has proper paperwork/warrants, the protocol is to turn
               them away from dorms (different situation with warrant)
            3. ICE has not communicated interest in coming to campus
        2. AI cameras
            1. MITPD works with IS\&T in using cameras, but AI features are not
               currently in use
            2. could change in future with certain features (slip and fall
               recognition, backpack/unattended item recognition)
            3. footage from cameras does not go into any other database besides
               security’s for 30 days
                1. if any agency wants access to the footage, would need
                   subpoena
            4. plans for more camera installations on campus
                1. want to cover remaining blank areas; Ring cameras played a
                   part in identifying Brown shooter until Watertown, so thought
                   is cameras can provide important info to security
            5. cameras are supposedly not used as evidence in student
               disciplinary matters unless they involve potentially criminal
               activities
                1. can include tagging, possibly hacking if involving breaking &
                   entering etc etc
            6. concerning protests
                1. large gathering must be filmed per POST (police policy), but
                   cameras are not used for disciplinary purposes
        3. Brown incident prompts question of MIT response time in similar
           situation; active shooter drills
            1. if there was active shooter call in the station (either from an
               officer or many calls at once), emergency button sends canned
               message to students/staff → so nearly immediate response
            2. officer dispatch, response time is under 3 minutes
            3. active shooter drills might become mandatory
                1. currently voluntary trainings are available with PD or
                   emergency management
                2. rolling these out is slow, pushing for mandatory training
        4. what do patrols look like
            1. want them to be visibly invisible, helping out where needed but
               not intrusive
            2. can join a ride-along if you want
        5. will MIT publicly disclose future camera locations
            1. can’t speak for the administration
