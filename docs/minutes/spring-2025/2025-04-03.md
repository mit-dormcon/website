### Student Leadership

- Talking briefly about student leadership in the abstract
- Particularly regarding discretion
    - When you get information from administrators, what are best practices for
      when you share information, when you choose to get community feedback,
      when you keep things close to your chest as a student leader
    - Jordan \- for me, if something is not super time-sensitive, and I don’t
      have all the information, and people will have questions that I don’t have
      the answer too, keep info close to chest till there’s more info
    - when you’re on a committee, say, or having meetings with admin, as part of
      job as student leader, if big news gets dropped (e.g., in a meeting with
      HRS, they say they’re going to demolish every dorm), ask the question “can
      I tell people?”
    - student leaders get info that is not finalized, which is really useful for
      planning, can think about how dorms would react, how it can be implemented
      in a good way, if it can’t be shared (not final) \- don’t want to share it
      on the side, where it gets made public, and people start having opinions
      when it’s not necessarily going to happen
        - can cause panic and chaos, also makes student leaders look bad to
          admin
    - Geoffrey: “Jordan, you’re a fucking idiot, that’s wrong” /s
        - has learned from Institute committees, if there are issues that can’t
          be discussed with wider student body, want to get more info about what
          students would think about a certain thing, would try listening to
          meeting and figuring out what groups of people already know
        - e.g., if HRS is discussing demolishing all dorms :( \- guessing that
          Jordan and Ananda would know something
        - limiting discussion to groups that would already know
